We live in a fragmented world

A world that is plagued by disconnection, distraction, and injustice. {# But there is hope, there is another way. #}

And in this world we struggle

With high rates of poverty, ongoing racial segregation, underinvestment in our schools, and many other issues.

But there is HOPE

Because we choose not to hide, but to dwell in this struggle, because of the tremendous assets these places possess.

To create an alternate way of life

That is centered, whole, founded on kinship and love, and rooted in discipleship.

We are the Family Tree

"We believe the people and places surrounding the Enderly Park neighborhood are the good soil in which we desire to be planted. Because the soil where we are planted bears the wounds of racial oppression, we will always be working against the legacy of racism. We look to our neighbors for wisdom, guidance, and mutual care."
